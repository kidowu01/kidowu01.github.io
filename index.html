Biography

Leading AI-Driven Drug Discovery

Dr. Kehinde Ademola Idowu is a recognized molecular virologist, biochemist, and computational translational scientist with over 15 years of interdisciplinary expertise in drug discovery, infectious disease modeling, AI-driven therapeutics, and global health innovation. He holds a Ph.D. in Virology from the University of KwaZulu-Natal, South Africa, and currently serves as Research Assistant Professor in the Department of Pharmaceutical Sciences at Texas Southern University (TSU).

As the Director of both the Infectious Disease Drug Discovery Laboratory (IDDD Lab) and the Biomolecular Research and Advanced Computing Centre (BioRACC) at TSU, Dr. Idowu leads cutting-edge programs integrating computational biology, machine learning, molecular dynamics, and experimental validation for antiviral, antibacterial, and anticancer drug discovery. He has pioneered the application of high-performance computing clusters and AI-based pipelines for the rational design and simulation of novel therapeutics targeting co-infections such as HIV/TB, SARS-CoV-2, and other emerging pathogens.

Dr. Idowu's research is backed by major funding agencies, including the NIH, HRSA, and the U.S. Department of Energy. Notably, he is the Principal Investigator (PI) on several federally funded projects, such as a $2.4M HRSA award addressing maternal and child health disparities, and a Department of Energy grant focused on pandemic biopreparedness. He has authored more than 30 peer-reviewed publications and contributed extensively to the fields of protein interaction modeling, computational biology, and pharmacogenomics.
